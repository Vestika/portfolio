## Classic Books

### The Design of Everyday Things
**Author**: Don Norman
**Year**: 1988 (revised 2013)
**Why read it**: Foundational text that coined the term "user experience." Explains how design principles apply to everyday objects and interfaces.

**Key concepts**:
- Affordances and signifiers
- Feedback and constraints
- Mental models
- Human error and system design

**Best for**: Understanding core design principles, learning to think like a designer

---

### Don't Make Me Think
**Author**: Steve Krug
**Year**: 2000 (3rd edition 2014)
**Why read it**: Practical guide to web usability with clear, actionable advice.

**Key concepts**:
- Web usability principles
- Navigation design
- Usability testing on a budget
- Mobile usability

**Best for**: Web designers, product managers, anyone building websites

---

### Hooked: How to Build Habit-Forming Products
**Author**: Nir Eyal
**Year**: 2014
**Why read it**: Explains the psychology behind habit-forming products.

**Key concepts**:
- The Hook Model (trigger, action, variable reward, investment)
- Building habits through product design
- Ethical considerations
- Trigger types (external, internal)

**Best for**: Product designers, product managers, startup founders

---

### Atomic Design
**Author**: Brad Frost
**Year**: 2016
**Why read it**: Introduces a methodology for creating design systems.

**Key concepts**:
- Atoms, molecules, organisms, templates, pages
- Component-based design
- Design systems
- Pattern libraries

**Best for**: Frontend developers, design system creators, UI designers

**Free online**: https://atomicdesign.bradfrost.com/

---

### Lean UX
**Authors**: Jeff Gothelf & Josh Seiden
**Year**: 2013 (3rd edition 2021)
**Why read it**: Integrates UX with Agile/Lean methodologies.

**Key concepts**:
- Outcomes over output
- Cross-functional collaboration
- Continuous discovery and validation
- Minimum viable product (MVP)

**Best for**: Product teams, UX designers in Agile environments, product managers

## Modern Books & Guides

### Refactoring UI
**Authors**: Adam Wathan & Steve Schoger
**Year**: 2018
**Why read it**: Practical visual design tips for developers.

**Key concepts**:
- Visual hierarchy without relying on font size
- Working with color and whitespace
- Typography best practices
- Practical examples and before/after comparisons

**Best for**: Developers learning design, UI designers

**Website**: https://www.refactoringui.com/

---

### Form Design Patterns
**Author**: Adam Silver
**Year**: 2018
**Why read it**: Comprehensive guide to designing accessible, usable forms.

**Key concepts**:
- Form validation patterns
- Error message design
- Progressive enhancement
- Accessibility in forms

**Best for**: Frontend developers, UX designers working on forms

---

### Laws of UX
**Author**: Jon Yablonski
**Year**: 2020
**Why read it**: Explains psychological principles applied to UX design.

**Key concepts**:
- Fitts's Law, Hick's Law, Jakob's Law
- Psychology of design decisions
- Practical applications
- Visual examples

**Best for**: UX designers, product designers, anyone interested in psychology of design

**Free online**: https://lawsofux.com/

## Recommended Reading Order

### For Beginners
1. Start with: **Don't Make Me Think** (Steve Krug) - Easy, practical
2. Then: **The Design of Everyday Things** (Don Norman) - Foundational principles
3. Follow with: **Laws of UX** (Jon Yablonski) - Psychology principles
4. Study: **WCAG 2.1 Guidelines** - Accessibility basics
5. Practice: Follow Nielsen Norman Group articles

### For Intermediate Designers
1. **Atomic Design** (Brad Frost) - Design systems
2. **Hooked** (Nir Eyal) - Engagement patterns
3. **Refactoring UI** (Wathan & Schoger) - Visual design polish
4. **Form Design Patterns** (Adam Silver) - Form expertise
5. **Lean UX** (Gothelf & Seiden) - Process and collaboration

### For Advanced Practitioners
- Nielsen Norman Group research reports
- Academic papers on HCI (Human-Computer Interaction)
- Specific domain resources (mobile, AR/VR, voice, AI)
- Attend conferences: UXDX, Interaction, UX London
